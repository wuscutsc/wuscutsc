Finding New Horizons in Education

Empowering refugees through education to build sustainable futures.

Since 1987

Since its inception in 1987, WUSC UTSC has made remarkable contributions to the lives of refugees through the Student Refugee Program. Each year, the committee has successfully sponsored one student, meaning that as of this year, WUSC UTSC has sponsored a total of 37 students.

Each of these students has traveled a profound journey, from uncertain futures in refugee camps to realizing their dreams of higher education and a promising future in Canada. The sponsorship of these students goes beyond mere financial assistance; it involves a comprehensive support system tailored to address educational, social, and integration needs within the UTSC campus and the wider community.

About WUSC

WUSC (World University Service of Canada) is a Canadian international development non-profit organization dedicated to improving education, employment, and empowerment opportunities for youth, women, and refugees in more than 25 countries around the world. For over fifty years, we have empowered our network of post-secondary institutions, private sector partners, and volunteers to help build a more equitable and sustainable world for youth. To find out more, please visit wusc.ca.

Students Testimonials

Bishar Adam Salat

Former SRP Former student, Masters in degree in Industrial relations and Human Resources – University of Toronto

Joining the Student Refugee Program was the gateway to transforming my life and career. After fleeing conflict in my homeland, I was given the opportunity to pursue higher education at the University of Toronto, where I completed my Master's in Industrial Relations and Human Resources. This program didn't just educate me; it sculpted my ability to empathize, strategize, and lead within the complex landscapes of global corporations. Today, as an HR professional at Amazon, I implement innovative workforce solutions that not only enhance productivity but also ensure an inclusive work environment. My role allows me to give back, creating policies that support diversity and inclusion—a testament to the values instilled in me through the SRP.

Chol Abit Bior

Student, University of Toronto Scarborough

Growing up, my fascination with how life works at a molecular level was just a distant dream, as access to education in my home country was disrupted by ongoing conflicts and instability. The opportunity to study Biochemistry at UTSC has been nothing short of transformative. The rigorous academic environment, coupled with the support from the WUSC Local Committee, has not only helped my scientific enthusiasm but also empowered me as an advocate for global health issues. I'm currently involved in research aimed at developing cheaper, more effective drug therapies for diseases that plague my home region.
